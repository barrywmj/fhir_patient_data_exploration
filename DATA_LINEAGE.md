## Diagnosis-Anchored Outcome Definition

For disease-scoped outcomes, the baseline value means:

> First valid numeric measurement on or after the first qualifying diagnosis date.

The latest value means:

> Latest valid numeric measurement after that same diagnosis anchor.

An improver is a patient whose latest value moved from baseline in the favorable direction by at least the measure-specific threshold.

A non-improver is a patient whose value worsened, stayed flat, or improved by less than the threshold.

General baseline measures such as body weight, heart rate, and respiratory rate use first valid measurement as baseline because they are broad monitoring signals rather than diagnosis-specific outcomes.
